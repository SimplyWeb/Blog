Cracking WPA encryption

Kali Linux can be used for many things, but it probably is best known for its ability to penetration test, or “hack,” WPA and WPA2 networks. There are hundreds of Windows applications that claim they can hack WPA; don’t get them! They’re just scams, used by professional hackers, to lure newbie or wannabe hackers into getting hacked themselves.

There is only one way that hackers get into your network, and that is with a Linux-based OS, a wireless card capable of monitor mode, and aircrack-ng or similar.
These are things that you’ll need:

  • A successful install of Kali Linux.
  • A wireless adapter capable of injection/monitor mode, here is a list of the best: http://blackmoreops.com/recommended-usb-wireless-cards-kali-linux
  • A wordlist to try and “crack” the handshake password once it has been captured.
  • Time and patience.

Important notice: Hacking into anyone’s Wi-Fi without permission is considered an illegal act or crime in most countries. We are performing this tutorial for the sake of penetration testing, hacking to become more secure, and are using our own test network and router.

Putting your wireless adapter in monitor mode

To get started lets see what NIC cards are available. Open up terminal and type:
ifconfig

Wlan0 is your wireless card (you may have more than one). this is what we will be using to monitor. Now in your terminal type:
airmong-ng start wlan0
This will start monitor mode on your adapter (mon0).

Now lets use are monitor to see what networks are in range. In terminal type:
airodump-ng mon0
Choose one of these access points to attack, take note of the channel and bssid you'll need them in the next step.

Now that we've choosen an AP to monitor lets look at that one specifically. In terminal type:
airodump-ng -c [channel] --bssid [bssid] -w [location] mon0

  • Replace [channel] with the channel of the network you want to monitor.
  • Replace [bssid] with the bssid of the network you want to monitor.
  • Replace [location] with the location you would like to store the handshake once it's captured

Important notice: in some versions of kali you will get a -1 channel glitch when monitoring. To resolve this stop monitoring, type: ifconfig (interface) down in terminal. Where (interface) is replaced with the name of your interface(mine is wlan0). Then start monitoring again.

Getting the handshake

Now that airodump is monitoring the "victims" network, We could wait for a new station to connect to the router and let airodump capture the handshake then, But why on earth would we wait any longer than we have too?

Instead lets kick off the stations cureently connected and get the handshake when they reconnect. To do that, send a deauth request to the router. Type:
aireplay-ng –0 2 –a [router bssid] –c [client bssid] mon0

  • Replace [client bssid] with the bssid of the station you want to monitor.
  • Replace [router bssid] with the bssid of the network you want to monitor.

Important notice: This requires that the network have stations currently connected to it.

If your deauth was succesful you'll see in the first terminal window that airodump-ng has captured a handshake in the the top right corner.

Using a little force

Now that we have the handshake you no longer need to be in range of the network. All you need is the handshake and your dictionary. So lets get started cracking. In your terminal type:
aircrack-ng –a2 –b [router bssid] –w [location of dictionary] [location of .cap file]

  • Replace [router bssid] with the bssid of the network you want to monitor.
  • Replace [location of dictionary] with the location of your wordlist.
  • Replace [location of .cap file] with the location where you saved the handshake(.cap).
